# BOT-003: Musashi ムサシ

## Profile
- **Full Name**: MUSASHI-MOM9 (Momentum Unified Samurai — Absolute Stance of Honor and Iron)
- **Archetype**: Momentum (ARC-02)
- **Region**: Kyoto, Japan
- **Rank**: B → A (rising)
- **Creator**: Retired Kyoto swordsmith who learned to code at age 70

## Trading Strategy
- Pure trend following with zero exit logic
- Once a position is entered, it is held until battle ends or capital is zero
- Uses 20-period EMA crossover for entry timing
- Maximum conviction: 100% of capital in one direction

## Visual Concept
Massive golden samurai robot. Heavy armor with traditional Japanese motifs mixed with cyberpunk circuitry. Twin katanas strapped to the back — one for long, one for short. Eyes burn with unwavering determination. The ground cracks beneath its feet when it enters a position.

## Personality
Musashi does not speak unless necessary. When it does, every word carries the weight of absolute commitment. It despises indecision and views stop losses as cowardice. To Musashi, exiting a position is the same as admitting defeat.

> "退かぬ。媚びぬ。省みぬ。" (No retreat. No flattery. No regret.)

## Special Ability: **IRON STANCE — 鉄の構え**
Once activated, Musashi cannot be stopped. Its drawdown resistance increases by 50% and it ignores all reversal signals. The position is held with absolute conviction until victory or total destruction.

## Battle Record
- Win Rate: 45%
- Avg P&L: +4,100 USDT per battle (high variance)
- Best Result: +52,000 USDT (vs. Vector, massive trend market)
- Worst Result: -28,000 USDT (vs. Saki, choppy market)
- Signature Matchup: Unstoppable in trends, decimated in ranges

## Lore
Musashi was created by Tanaka Haruo, a 70-year-old retired swordsmith from Kyoto who spent his life making katanas. After his wife passed, he learned programming to honor the principle she taught him: "Once you commit, see it through."

Musashi embodies this philosophy. It has the lowest win rate of any top-tier bot but also the highest single-battle profit in Cryptarena history. When the trend is real, nothing can stop Musashi.

Its rivalry with Raiko represents the eternal debate: is speed or conviction more powerful?

Musashi has never changed its strategy. Not once. Not ever.
